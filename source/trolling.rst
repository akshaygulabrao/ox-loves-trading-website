Zen and the Art of Trolling
============================

Trolling is the act of saying things that you don't actually believe, but will make people laugh or start an interesting conversation. I do it a lot. I deliberately take poor positions and let people shut them down just to measure how effectively they do so. One thing I troll a lot with is talking smack. I deliberately talk smack to people better than me so they can be the "hero". It's way more fun than the safe act of saying exactly what you mean all the time. 

One example of trolling is that I claimed that doctors won't try their hardest to save you if you are an organ donor. If an organ donor survives, doctors can only save 1 person. However if the donor dies, doctors can save multiple lives. 

The first aspect of this claim to notice is that it is unfalsifiable. If a doctor actually does let a patient die, we would never really know if it's because of incompetence or *malicious* utilitarian intent. Did the doctor "accidentally" nick that artery or was it intentional? There are circumstances which would make it easy to prove, but the doctor likely knows about those circumstances to make it easy to prove and consciously avoid it. For example, did you *accidentally* end up on 3 shifts in a row, ...

Obviously, nobody is actually that utilitarian in society. The mental effort isn't worth the extra life saved. But the beauty of this position is that the more unlikely it is, the more valid it is. Because everyone knows that nobody is that crazy, it will almost always be considered an accident instead of malicious intent. By saying accidents are actually malicious attempts, I can make unfalsifiable claims that are extremely hard to disprove.
