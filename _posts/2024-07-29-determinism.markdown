I used to think that I had a lot of control over my life. That somewhere after getting medically disqualified from attending the United States Air Force Academy, my life went down the drain. I eventually realized that not getting into the USAFA was good because I really wasn't a good fit for military service. It would have taked away my openess to ideas and I would have lost so many nights from browsing the internet, which has taught me a great deal about life.

One of the main things I learned on one of these nights was about determinism. It didn't really hit me at first. Like I understood that I am not fully in control of my life, but I didn't understand it's implications. I carried myself with a sense of agency, like whether things move for or against me had to do with me.
