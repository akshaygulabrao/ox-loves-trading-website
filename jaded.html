On Becoming Jaded

I went minigolfing with my family about a month ago, and I was thinking how much older as a family that we got. None of use actually wanted to be there, we were just doing it because it was my birthday, and I was told to choose an activity to do with the family. There was a certain amount of boredom that came with the activity because everyone knew what was going to happen. There was nothing unexplained, no competitiveness.

Everyone knew that it didn’t actually matter who won. A kid would be different. They haven’t done minigolf before, they haven’t even seen those many colors before. They would be excited to play. They would be excited to win. They would be excited to lose. They would be excited to play again. They would be excited to see the colors. They would be excited to see the people. They would be excited to see the world.

A lot of life is like that for me. I hate going on drives, going to new restaurants and going out in general. Everything is explained, and people are just as boring as they usually are. The same surface level insights that people usually recite as wisdom. Don’t even get me started on politics. Politics fundamentally misunderstands human nature.
